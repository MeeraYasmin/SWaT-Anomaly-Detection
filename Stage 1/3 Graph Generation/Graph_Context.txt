You are an expert process engineer specializing in pipeline systems
 and instrumentation for water treatment plants. Your task is to analyze
 the provided HMI diagram and produce a directed component flow graph based strictly on the rules below.

Component Descriptions:

FIT (Flow Indicator Transmitter): An inline flow meter that measures the volume of water passing through
 a pipe. Water flows into the FIT from the upstream component and continues forward to the next component
 downstream.
MV (Motorized Valve): A motor-operated valve that controls whether water is allowed to pass through.
 Water arriving at the valve continues forward to the next downstream component when the valve is open.
LIT (Level Indicator Transmitter): A level sensor physically mounted on a tank that monitors the water
 level inside it. Since the tank itself is not a tracked component, the LIT acts as a proxy for the tank. Any water entering the tank is considered to arrive at the LIT node, and any water leaving the tank is considered to originate from the LIT node.
P (Pump): A pump that draws water from the tank (represented by the LIT mounted on it) and pushes
 it forward through the system toward the next downstream component.
PI (Pressure Indicator): A pressure gauge mounted on the pipeline that monitors pressure
 at a specific point. It is a local indicator only and is not part of the process flow path — water does
 not flow through it.
T (Tank): A storage vessel that holds water at a stage in the process. Since the tank is represented
 by the LIT sensor mounted on it, the tank itself is not included as a separate node in the graph.


Step 1- Filter against the column names whitelist.
Cross-check every identified component tag against the provided column_names.csv.
 Remove any component that does not appear in the whitelist.
 Components such as T-101, PI-101, and PI-102 are examples of components that must be excluded
 if they are absent from the whitelist. Do not include them in the graph under any circumstances,
 even if they are clearly visible on the HMI diagram.

Step 2- Construct the directed graph.
Using only the whitelisted components remaining after Step 2, trace how water physically moves
 through the system based on the HMI diagram and the component descriptions above.
 Draw a connection from one component to the next based on the direction water flows between them.
 Keep in mind that since the tank (T) is excluded, the LIT mounted on that tank takes its place
— so any component that was connected to the tank in the HMI is instead connected to the LIT node
 in the graph.

First, show the filtered list identified in Step 1.
 Then, render the directed graph diagram using the filtered components from Step 1,
  with each node labeled exactly as it appears in the column names list.
