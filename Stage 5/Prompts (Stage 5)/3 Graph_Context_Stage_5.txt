Component Descriptions:

FIT (Flow Indicator Transmitter): An inline flow meter that measures the volume of water passing through
 a pipe. Water flows into the FIT from the upstream component and continues forward to the next component
 downstream.
P (Pump): A pump that draws water from the tank (represented by the LIT mounted on it) and pushes
 it forward through the system toward the next downstream component.
PI (Pressure Indicator): A pressure gauge mounted on the pipeline that monitors pressure
 at a specific point. It is a local indicator only and is not part of the process flow path — water does
 not flow through it.
AIT (Analyser Indicator Transmitter): An inline water quality sensor that measures a specific chemical property of the water such as pH, ORP, or conductivity. It is a local indicator only and is not part of the process flow path — water does not flow through it.
MV (Motorized Valve): A motor-operated valve that controls whether water is allowed to pass through.
 Water arriving at the valve continues forward to the next downstream component when the valve is open.
FI (Flow Indicator / Rotameter): A local visual flow indicator that shows the flow rate on-site only. It has no electrical output and does not transmit data to the control system — it is a local indicator only and is not part of the process flow path. Water does not flow through it.
PSH (Pressure Switch High): A binary pressure switch that triggers a high-pressure alarm and shuts down the UF feed pump when inlet pressure exceeds its set-point. It is a local protective device only and is not part of the process flow path — water does not flow through it.

PSL (Pressure Switch Low): A binary pressure switch mounted on the high pressure pump suction header that triggers a low-pressure alarm and shuts down the RO feed pump when the incoming pressure drops below its set-point, protecting the pump from running dry. It is a local protective device only and is not part of the process flow path — water does not flow through it.
PIT (Pressure Indicator Transmitter): A pressure transmitter that continuously monitors and transmits the pipeline pressure at a specific point to the PLC and HMI. In P5, PIT-501 monitors RO feed pressure, PIT-502 monitors RO permeate pressure, and PIT-503 monitors RO reject pressure — together used for Trans-Membrane Pressure (TMP) trending. It is a local indicator only and is not part of the process flow path — water does not flow through it.
RO (Reverse Osmosis Membrane): A high-pressure membrane vessel that removes almost all impurities from the feed water, producing a permeate stream and a concentrate reject stream. Water flows into the RO membrane from the upstream component and continues forward to the next downstream component. Since RO is not included in the dataset, it is excluded from the flow graph.
